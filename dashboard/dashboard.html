The jump from Mid-level to Senior represents the largest relative increase at 36.5%, while Senior roles account for 46% of all positions.

Senior is the market. SE roles dominate at 46% of all jobs (280 positions) with a USD 138K average — the most liquid tier for career transitions.

3 — Salary Growth 2020–2022

Salaries grew 4% in 2021 then accelerated sharply to +20% in 2022, reflecting the post-pandemic surge in data talent demand.

Average salary in USD per year. Job count shown as secondary reference.

2022 inflection. The +20% jump from 2021 to 2022 is the sharpest growth in the dataset, coinciding with peak tech hiring demand.

Volume effect. Job records more than quadrupled from 72 (2020) to 318 (2022), suggesting broader dataset coverage and market expansion.

Dataset limit. The analysis covers 2020–2022 only. The 2023 market correction following tech layoffs is not represented.

:::

4 — Remote Work Patterns

Fully remote roles dominate at 63% and command the highest average salary at USD 122K — USD 16K above on-site equivalents.

Job share and average salary by remote work type.

Remote premium. Fully remote roles average USD 122K vs USD 106K for on-site — a 15% premium. This may reflect a seniority confound: senior roles are both better paid and more likely to be remote.

Hybrid underperforms. Hybrid roles average USD 81K, 28% below on-site — possibly reflecting a different role mix rather than a hybrid penalty per se.

:::

5 — Geographic Distribution

The US accounts for 67% of all records and leads on compensation at USD 144K average — 26% above Japan, the second highest-paying market.

Average salary in USD by company location. Only countries with ≥5 records shown.

US concentration. With 355 of 607 records, conclusions about non-US markets should be treated with caution — most countries have fewer than 30 data points.

Regional disparity. European salaries range from USD 52K (Greece) to USD 81K (Germany), while India ranks last at USD 29K — reflecting local purchasing power rather than skill gaps.
